Overage Model

Queue and Reserve share the same entry pool. Overage applies when combined Queue + Reserve entries exceed the monthly cap. Queue uses the same per-entry overage rate as Reserve.

SingaporeTaiwan
Monthly cap (Queue + Reserve shared)750 entries700 entries
Overage rateSGD$0.30/entryNT$3/entry
Notification cost at overageSGD$0.06 (WhatsApp)NT$1.00 (LINE)
Oddle margin per overage entrySGD$0.24NT$2.00

Overage rate is the same as Reserve — Queue notification cost (WhatsApp/LINE) is absorbed by Oddle within the overage margin. Bird calling is usage-based and charged separately regardless of cap status.

Decisions Required Before Launch

DecisionOptions / Context
WhatsApp & LINE notification cost — absorbed or usage-based? WhatsApp/LINE are mandatory for Queue (no email alternative). Absorbing mirrors how email is treated for Reserve. At cap: SGD$45/mo (SG), NT$700/mo (TW).
Entry cap — shared pool with Reserve, or separate Queue cap? Shared pool keeps it simple (750 SG / 700 TW combined). Separate cap avoids penalising merchants who use both products but increases cost exposure.
Overage rate — same as Reserve, or Queue-specific? Reserve rate: SGD$0.30/entry (SG), NT$3/entry (TW). Using the same rate is simple, but margin is lower than Reserve — Queue incurs notification cost per entry (SGD$0.06 WhatsApp / NT$1.00 LINE) that Reserve does not. A Queue-specific rate could preserve comparable margin.
Should manual (staff-entered) queue entries count toward the cap? Reserve does not charge for manual bookings. But unlike Reserve (email), Queue requires WhatsApp/LINE even for staff-entered entries — the customer still needs to be notified when their table is ready. This means every manual entry incurs a notification cost, which may justify counting them toward the cap and overage model. Decision needed on whether to treat manual Queue entries the same as QR entries, or exempt them (and absorb the notification cost).
Bird calling rate Usage-based, charged separately to merchants. Rate not yet confirmed.
