Queue Launch Plan

Singapore & Taiwan  ·  April 2026  ·  Kate Ong

Why Now

Queue management is an adjacent TAM expansion for Oddle. Most restaurants with high walk-in demand are managing queues manually today — paper numbers, staff calling names, no customer data captured. By bundling queue management into the All Access plan, Oddle can:

  • Attract net new merchants for whom queue management is a primary pain point
  • Deepen value for existing merchants, reducing churn and increasing platform stickiness
  • Build a richer customer database by capturing walk-in diner identity via Oddle Pass

What We're Launching

Phase 1 QR-based queue — customers scan at venue to join digitally, then walk around freely until notified via WhatsApp (SG/MY) or LINE (TW). Queue is bundled into the All Access plan — no standalone SKU.

Phase 2 Virtual queue (remote join before arriving) — not in current scope.

Kiosk is not in scope.

Merchant Value Proposition

Phase 1 QR Launch

  • Capture demand you're currently losing — customers who see a long physical queue and walk away now join digitally instead. Queue recovers that lost revenue.
  • Better customer experience, less attrition — customers are freed from standing in line. They scan, walk around the mall, and return when notified. Fewer walk-aways, less frustration.
  • Every walk-in becomes a known customer — Oddle Pass login is mandatory to join the queue, so every queue entry creates a verified customer profile in CDP. Walk-in diners are no longer anonymous.
  • Better value with All Access — Queue is included in the All Access plan, which also covers ordering, reservations, and loyalty. For existing merchants, activation requires no new contract. For new merchants, All Access is a compelling entry point vs. competitors who charge for queue management alone.

Phase 2 Virtual Queue (Next Phase)

  • Destination restaurants — customers can check wait times before leaving home and decide when to go. Removes the risk of travelling to find a long queue.
  • Demand curve smoothing — customers with timing flexibility can self-select to arrive off-peak once they can see real-time wait times remotely. Over time this can flatten the lunch/dinner bottleneck.

How We Find Them

Pilot merchants will be hand-selected by the front office team — goal is to validate the feature and surface major issues, not maximise coverage. The scoring models below are for scaling sales and upsell outreach post-pilot.

Pilot Merchant Profile

  • Genuine peak-hour queue pressure in a mall — hot pot, BBQ, popular casual dining
  • Existing CS relationship — responsive owner/manager for quick feedback
  • Mid-volume — est. 100–400 queue entries/month
  • Single outlet preferred — fewer variables; may be difficult given most target merchants are mall-based chains
  • Include at least one net new merchant — to validate the full onboarding flow, not just activation
